Exponential Search

Double the range until you overshoot, then binary search the pocket.

Exponential search finds the range where the target lies by jumping at positions 1, 2, 4, 8, 16… until it either goes out of bounds or finds an element greater than the target. It then applies binary search within the range [prev, curr] — a much smaller window.

Imagine searching for a word in a massive sorted dictionary. Instead of opening to the middle (which may be very far from your target), you first flip to page 1, then 2, then 4, then 8… until you overshoot. You now know the word is somewhere between the previous stop and the overshoot. Binary search in that narrow range is fast.

This makes exponential search especially powerful for unbounded or infinite sorted arrays where the size is unknown, and for cases where the target is near the beginning of a large array. Its time complexity is O(log n) — dominated by the binary search phase, but with a small constant thanks to quick range-finding.

Time Complexity
O(log n)
Space Complexity
O(1)
Best For
Unbounded arrays
