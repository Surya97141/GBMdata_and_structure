Binary Search

Halve the search space at every step — logarithmic elegance.

Binary search works on sorted arrays. It compares the target against the middle element, then discards the half where the target cannot be. Each comparison eliminates 50% of remaining candidates, achieving O(log n) in at most ⌈log₂(n+1)⌉ comparisons.

The classic analogy: a phone book. Looking for "Smith", open to the middle — "M" section. Smith comes after M, discard the left half. Repeat until found.

Time
O(log n)
Space
O(1)
Requires
Sorted array
