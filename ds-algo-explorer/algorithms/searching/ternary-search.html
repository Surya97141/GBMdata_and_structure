Ternary Search

Divide into three parts using two midpoints — eliminate a third each step.

Ternary Search divides the search range into three parts with two midpoints: m1 = lo + (hi-lo)/3 and m2 = hi - (hi-lo)/3. Compare the target with both; eliminate one-third of the range per iteration. Complexity is O(log3 n).

Despite 2 comparisons per step vs 1 for Binary Search, ternary search has no practical advantage on arrays. It is most valuable for unimodal functions (finding a maximum or minimum in continuous domains), where binary search cannot apply.
