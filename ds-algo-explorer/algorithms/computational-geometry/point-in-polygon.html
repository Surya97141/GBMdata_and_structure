Point in Polygon

Ray casting: shoot a ray rightward and count how many polygon edges it crosses — odd means inside.

Imagine standing at a point and shooting a laser beam infinitely to the right. If the beam crosses the polygon boundary an odd number of times, you're inside; an even number means you're outside. This is the ray casting algorithm, also called the crossing number test.

For each edge of the polygon, we check whether the horizontal ray from our test point intersects it. We only count intersections where the edge crosses the ray's horizontal level (one vertex above, one below) and the intersection's x-coordinate is to the right of our point.

Used everywhere: determining if a GPS coordinate is inside a country boundary, hit-testing in UI frameworks (is your click inside a widget?), flood-fill operations in image editors, and geographic information systems to assign points to regions.
