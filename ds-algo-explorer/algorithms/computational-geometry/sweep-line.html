Sweep Line

Process geometric events left-to-right with a vertical line — turning O(n²) problems into O(n log n).

Picture a vertical line sweeping from left to right across a plane. As it crosses the left endpoint of a segment, we add that segment to an active set; when it crosses the right endpoint, we remove it. At any moment, only currently-intersected segments need to be compared with their neighbors — dramatically reducing work.

The sweep line technique solves interval overlap counting, area of union of rectangles, line-segment intersection (Bentley–Ottmann), and closest-pair detection. The key insight: sort all endpoints (events) by x-coordinate and process them in order, maintaining a balanced BST of active segments ordered by y-coordinate.

This simulation shows counting overlapping intervals: add a set of horizontal intervals, sweep left-to-right, and at each event record how many intervals are active. The maximum active count is the answer.
