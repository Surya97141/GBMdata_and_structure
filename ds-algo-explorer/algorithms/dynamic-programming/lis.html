Longest Increasing Subsequence

Find the longest subsequence of numbers in strictly increasing order.

Think of stock prices over time. You want to find the longest streak of days where each day's price was higher than the previous one you picked — but you can skip days in between. That's the LIS problem.

The O(n²) DP approach: dp[i] is the length of the longest increasing subsequence ending at index i. For each i, look back at all j<i where a[j] < a[i] and take the maximum dp[j]+1. The answer is the maximum value in dp[].

An O(n log n) solution using patience sorting exists too, but the DP version is more intuitive and easier to visualise. After computing dp[], backtracing parent pointers gives the actual subsequence.
