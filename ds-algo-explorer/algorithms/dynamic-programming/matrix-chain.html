Matrix Chain Multiplication

Optimal parenthesization to minimize scalar multiplications.

Given a chain of matrices A₁×A₂×...×Aₙ, find the parenthesization that minimizes the total number of scalar multiplications. dp[i][j] = minimum cost to multiply matrices from i to j. Try all split points k: dp[i][j] = min over k of (dp[i][k] + dp[k+1][j] + p[i-1]×p[k]×p[j]), where p[] are the dimensions. The DP fills diagonals of increasing chain length.

Classic interval DP. The optimal order can differ massively from left-to-right — for example, A(1×100)×B(100×1)×C(1×100) costs 200 multiplications in one order, but 1+10,000 in another.

Time
O(n³)
Space
O(n²)
