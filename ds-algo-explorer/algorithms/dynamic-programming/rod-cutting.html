Rod Cutting

Maximize profit by cutting a rod into pieces sold at different prices.

Imagine you own a steel rod of length n and a price list that tells you how much a piece of each length is worth. You can make as many cuts as you like, anywhere along the rod. The goal is to figure out the cutting strategy that maximizes total revenue.

A greedy approach (always cut the highest-ratio piece first) fails. Dynamic programming saves us: dp[i] stores the maximum revenue from a rod of length i. To fill it, we try every first-cut size j from 1 to i and take max(price[j] + dp[i-j]).

This bottom-up DP runs in O(n²) time — each subproblem is solved once and its result reused, turning a naively exponential search into a fast polynomial one.
