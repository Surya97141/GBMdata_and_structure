Egg Drop Problem

Minimum trials needed in the worst case to find the critical floor.

Given e eggs and n floors, find the minimum number of trials (in the worst case) to determine the critical floor above which eggs break. dp[e][f] = minimum trials needed with e eggs and f floors. Try each floor k: dp[e][f] = 1 + min over k of max(dp[e-1][k-1], dp[e][f-k]). The max captures worst case (egg breaks vs doesn't break).

If the egg breaks: look below (dp[e-1][k-1]). If it doesn't: look above (dp[e][f-k]). We minimize the worst case over all choices of k. With 1 egg: always dp[1][f]=f (must test every floor). With ∞ eggs: dp[∞][f]=⌈log₂(f+1)⌉ (binary search).

Time
O(e · n²)
1 egg
n trials
∞ eggs
log₂n trials
Worst case
