Rat in a Maze

Find a path through a grid maze from top-left to bottom-right, navigating around walls using backtracking DFS.

A rat starts at cell (0,0) and must reach cell (N-1,N-1). Some cells are blocked (walls). The rat can move right or down (or in all 4 directions in extended versions). Backtracking explores each path and retreats when it hits a dead end.

Think of navigating a physical maze: you walk forward, mark your trail, and when you hit a dead end you retrace your steps to the last junction and try a different direction. The algorithm does exactly this, making the explored-and-failed cells visually distinguishable from the current active path.

Click any cell to toggle it as a wall (dark) or open (light). The start (0,0) and end (7,7) cannot be walled. Then press Solve to watch the backtracking DFS in action.
