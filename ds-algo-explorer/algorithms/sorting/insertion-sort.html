Insertion Sort

Build the sorted array one card at a time — just like sorting your hand of playing cards.

Insertion sort builds the final sorted array one element at a time. It takes each element from the unsorted portion and inserts it into the correct position within the already-sorted left portion, shifting larger elements one step to the right to make room.

Think of how you sort playing cards in your hand. You pick up one card at a time from the table and slide it into the right position among the cards you're already holding. After you've picked up all the cards, your hand is sorted. The left part (held cards) is always sorted; the right part (cards on the table) is unsorted.

Insertion sort is very efficient for small datasets and nearly-sorted arrays, where it achieves O(n) in the best case. It is adaptive — it skips unnecessary work when elements are already in place. Insertion sort is stable and is used in practice as the base case in Timsort and Introsort.
