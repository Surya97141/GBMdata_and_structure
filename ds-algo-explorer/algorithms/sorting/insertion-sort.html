Insertion Sort

Build the sorted array one element at a time — like sorting playing cards in your hand.

Insertion Sort picks each element from the unsorted portion (the "key") and inserts it at the correct position in the already-sorted prefix. Elements larger than the key shift right to make room.

It runs in O(n) on nearly-sorted data and is the fastest simple sort for small arrays. Most production sort implementations (Timsort, Introsort) switch to Insertion Sort for partitions smaller than ~16 elements.
