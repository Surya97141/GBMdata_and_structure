Bubble Sort

The classic "let the biggest bubble to the top" algorithm.

Bubble sort works by repeatedly stepping through the list, comparing adjacent elements and swapping them if they are in the wrong order. The pass through the list is repeated until the list is sorted. It gets its name because smaller elements "bubble" to the top of the list — or equivalently, larger elements "sink" to the bottom — with each pass.

Think of it like sorting a row of numbered blocks by height. You walk from left to right and whenever you spot a tall block next to a shorter one, you swap them. After the first full walk, the tallest block is guaranteed to be at the far right. After the second walk, the second-tallest is in place, and so on.

Bubble sort is rarely used in production due to its O(n²) average complexity, but it is easy to understand and implement, making it a classic teaching tool. An optimised version can detect if no swaps occurred in a pass and terminate early — giving O(n) on already-sorted data.
