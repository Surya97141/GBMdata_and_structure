Pancake Sort

Only one operation allowed: flip the top k elements — like flipping pancakes.

Pancake Sort can only move elements using a "flip" operation: reverse the prefix of length k. To place the largest unsorted element in position: (1) find the max in the unsorted prefix, (2) flip to bring it to front, (3) flip the whole unsorted portion to place it at the end.

It is used in computer theory and interconnection networks where prefix-reversal is the only allowed operation. At most 2(n−1) flips are needed.

Time

Flips
≤ 2(n−1)
