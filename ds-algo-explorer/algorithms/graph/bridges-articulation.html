Bridges & Articulation Points

Find the critical edges and vertices whose removal disconnects the network.

A bridge is an edge whose removal increases the number of connected components in a graph — it's the only connection between two parts of the network. An articulation point (or cut vertex) is a node whose removal disconnects the graph. Finding these is crucial for network reliability analysis.

Tarjan's bridge-finding algorithm uses DFS and maintains two arrays: disc[] (discovery time) and low[] (lowest discovery time reachable). An edge (u,v) is a bridge if low[v] > disc[u] — meaning v can't reach u or its ancestors without going through u→v. Similarly, u is an articulation point if it's the root with ≥2 DFS children, or a non-root where some child v has low[v] ≥ disc[u].

Think of a city network: bridges are the single roads connecting city clusters, and articulation points are the junctions that, if blocked, isolate neighborhoods. Telecom engineers use this to identify single points of failure in fiber networks.
