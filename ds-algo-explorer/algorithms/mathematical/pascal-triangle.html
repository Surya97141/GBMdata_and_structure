Pascal's Triangle

An arithmetic triangle hiding Fibonacci numbers, powers of 2, Catalan numbers, and binomial coefficients.

Pascal's Triangle places 1's on the edges and fills each interior cell with the sum of the two cells above it. Row n contains the binomial coefficients C(n,0), C(n,1), ..., C(n,n). The triangle was known in China (Yang Hui, 1261) long before Pascal.

Hidden patterns abound: row sums are powers of 2 (Σ C(n,k) = 2^n); the shallow diagonals sum to Fibonacci numbers; the central column contains Catalan numbers (adjusted); coloring cells divisible by a prime p produces the Sierpinski triangle fractal.

Computationally, Pascal's triangle enables O(1) binomial coefficient queries after O(n²) preprocessing. Essential for dynamic programming (counting paths, coin change combinations) and probability theory (binomial distribution).
