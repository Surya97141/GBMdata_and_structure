Fermat's Little Theorem

If p is prime and gcd(a,p)=1, then a^(p-1) ≡ 1 (mod p).

Fermat's Little Theorem states that a^(p-1) ≡ 1 (mod p) when p is prime and gcd(a,p)=1. This means a^(-1) ≡ a^(p-2) (mod p) — giving a simple formula for modular inverse. It is also the basis for the Fermat primality test.
