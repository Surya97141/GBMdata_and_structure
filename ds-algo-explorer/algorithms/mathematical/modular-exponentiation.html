Modular Exponentiation

Compute base^exp mod m in O(log exp) using the square-and-multiply technique.

Naive computation of 2^1000 mod m would require 999 multiplications and huge intermediate numbers. Modular exponentiation solves this by exploiting the binary representation of the exponent: instead of multiplying 1000 times, we square and multiply at most 20 times (log₂ 1000 ≈ 10).

The algorithm reads the binary digits of the exponent from most significant to least significant. For each bit: always square the running value; if the bit is 1, also multiply by base. All operations reduce mod m, keeping numbers bounded.

This is the core operation of RSA encryption/decryption, Diffie-Hellman key exchange, and primality testing. Without it, public-key cryptography would be computationally infeasible.
