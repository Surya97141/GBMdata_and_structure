Sieve of Eratosthenes

Find all primes up to N by iteratively marking composites — ancient and brilliant.

Write all numbers from 2 to N. Circle 2 (it's prime), cross out every 2nd number after it. Circle the next uncrossed number (3), cross out multiples of 3. Repeat. What remains uncrossed at the end are exactly the primes up to N.

Key insight: every composite has a prime factor ≤ √N. So we only need to start crossing at p² (everything below p² was already crossed by smaller primes), and stop when p > √N. This gives O(N log log N) — remarkably fast.

Time
O(N log log N)
Space
O(N)
Invented
240 BC
