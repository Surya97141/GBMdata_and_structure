Euclidean GCD Algorithm

Greatest Common Divisor via repeated remainder — O(log min(a,b)).

gcd(a, b) = gcd(b, a mod b). When b=0, GCD = a. This works because any divisor of both a and b also divides (a mod b). Each step reduces the larger number to its remainder, converging extremely fast — O(log) steps.
