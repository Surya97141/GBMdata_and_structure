Catalan Numbers

A sequence counting valid bracket arrangements, binary trees, polygon triangulations, and dozens more combinatorial structures.

Catalan numbers C(n) = (2n choose n) / (n+1) = 1, 1, 2, 5, 14, 42, 132, 429, ... They appear in an extraordinary range of counting problems — all equivalent but superficially different. This "universality" makes them one of the most ubiquitous sequences in combinatorics.

The recurrence is C(0)=1, C(n+1) = Σ C(i)×C(n-i) for i=0..n. This is the convolution of the sequence with itself — reflecting how a structure of size n+1 splits into a left part of size i and a right part of size n-i.

Applications include: counting valid parenthesizations of n+1 factors, number of distinct BSTs with n keys, triangulations of a convex polygon with n+2 vertices, and monotonic lattice paths from (0,0) to (n,n) that don't cross the diagonal.
