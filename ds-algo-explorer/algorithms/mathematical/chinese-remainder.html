Chinese Remainder Theorem

Reconstruct a number from its remainders modulo pairwise coprime moduli — uniquely in O(n log n).

CRT says: if m₁, m₂, ..., mₖ are pairwise coprime, then for any remainders a₁, ..., aₖ, there exists a unique x (mod M = m₁×m₂×...×mₖ) satisfying all congruences simultaneously. The Sun Tzu algorithm (3rd century) first described this for troops counting.

The construction: M = product of all moduli. For each i, compute Mᵢ = M/mᵢ, then find yᵢ = Mᵢ⁻¹ mod mᵢ (using extended Euclidean). The solution is x = Σ(aᵢ × Mᵢ × yᵢ) mod M.

Used in RSA to speed up private key operations (working mod p and q separately), in error-correcting codes (Reed-Solomon), and in computer arithmetic for large integer multiplication.
