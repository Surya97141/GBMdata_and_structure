Job Sequencing with Deadlines

Maximize profit by scheduling unit-time jobs before their deadlines.

Each job takes exactly one unit of time, has a deadline (latest time-slot it can occupy), and yields a profit if completed. A single machine processes one job at a time, starting at time 0. How do you pick jobs to maximize total profit?

The greedy strategy: sort jobs by profit in descending order. For each job, try to place it in the latest available slot at or before its deadline. This greedy choice is optimal — by processing high-profit jobs first and assigning them to the last valid slot, we keep earlier slots free for other jobs.

The result is a schedule shown on a Gantt chart, with gaps shown as idle time if no job could fill a slot.
