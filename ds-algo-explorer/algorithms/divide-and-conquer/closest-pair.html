Divide & Conquer

Closest Pair of Points

Find the two nearest points in a plane in O(n log n) time.

The closest pair problem asks: given n points in a plane, which two are closest together? The naïve O(n²) solution checks every pair. The divide-and-conquer algorithm achieves O(n log n) by recursively finding the closest pair in the left and right halves, then checking a narrow strip around the dividing line for any cross-half closest pair.

The algorithm divides points by the median x-coordinate into left and right halves, recursively finds the minimum distance d in each half, then examines the strip of width 2d around the dividing line. A geometric argument shows that within this strip, each point need only be compared with at most 7 other points — keeping the strip check linear.

This algorithm is a beautiful example of D&C where the "combine" step requires careful geometric insight. The recurrence T(n) = 2T(n/2) + O(n log n) yields O(n log² n) without the strip optimization, and O(n log n) with it.
