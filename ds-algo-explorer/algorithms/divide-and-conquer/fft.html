Fast Fourier Transform

Compute DFT in O(n log n) using butterfly operations — the Cooley-Tukey algorithm.

The Cooley-Tukey FFT splits the DFT of n points into two n/2 DFTs of even and odd indices, then combines via butterfly: X[k] = E[k] + W^k × O[k] and X[k+n/2] = E[k] - W^k × O[k]. W = e^(-2πi/n) is the twiddle factor. Applied recursively: O(n log n) total.

Magnitude spectrum is shown. Yellow = twiddle factor combination. The butterfly network depth = log₂(n) levels.
