Binary Lifting

Precompute 2^j-th ancestors for each node to answer LCA and ancestor queries in O(log n) by jumping in powers of two.

Binary Lifting (also called sparse table on trees) preprocesses a table up[v][j] where up[v][j] is the 2^j-th ancestor of node v. To find the 5th ancestor, you decompose 5 = 4+1 = 2²+2⁰ and take two jumps instead of walking five steps. This reduces ancestor queries from O(n) to O(log n).

Think of an elevator that only stops at floors 1, 2, 4, 8, 16… Instead of pressing the button 13 times to reach floor 13, you press 8, then 4, then 1 — three presses. Binary lifting applies this idea to tree ancestors: represent any distance as a sum of powers of two, and precompute each "power-of-two jump" for every node.

For LCA queries: bring both nodes to the same depth using binary-lifting jumps, then simultaneously jump both up in powers of two until they would land on the same node. The answer is the parent of that landing point. This entire process takes O(log n) and makes binary lifting ideal for heavy competitive programming use cases.
