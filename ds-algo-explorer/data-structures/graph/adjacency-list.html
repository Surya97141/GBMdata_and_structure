Adjacency List

Each vertex stores a list of its neighbours — memory-efficient for sparse graphs and the go-to for BFS and DFS.

How it Works

An adjacency list is an array of lists. Index i holds all neighbours of vertex i. If A is connected to B and C, then list[A] = [B, C]. For undirected graphs both directions are stored. Adding a vertex is O(1); finding all neighbours is O(degree).

Think of a contact list in your phone: each person has their own list of contacts. Much more space-efficient than a full grid when most pairs are NOT connected — which is true of most real-world graphs like road networks or social graphs.
