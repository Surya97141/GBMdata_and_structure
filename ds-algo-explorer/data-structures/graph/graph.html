Graph

A non-linear data structure of vertices connected by edges — the backbone of networks, maps, and social graphs.

What is a Graph?

A graph is a collection of vertices (nodes) and edges (connections). Think of a road map: cities are vertices and roads are edges. Unlike trees, graphs have no root and can have cycles — roads can loop back to where you started.

Graphs can be directed (one-way streets) or undirected (two-way roads). They can be weighted (edge has a cost, like distance) or unweighted. Directed acyclic graphs (DAGs) underpin build systems, spreadsheets, and scheduling.

Real-world uses: Google Maps (shortest path), Facebook friends (undirected social graph), Git commits (DAG), airline routes, dependency resolution in package managers.
