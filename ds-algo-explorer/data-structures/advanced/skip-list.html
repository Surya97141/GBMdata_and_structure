Skip List

A probabilistic multi-level linked list enabling O(log n) search without balancing.

A Skip List is a randomized data structure that augments a sorted linked list with multiple levels of "express lanes." Level 0 contains all elements, while higher levels contain random subsets — roughly half at each successive level. To search for a value, you start at the top-left and jump forward, dropping a level when you overshoot.

The beauty of skip lists is that they achieve O(log n) average-case search, insertion, and deletion without the complex rotations needed by balanced BSTs like AVL or Red-Black trees. The trade-off is that the performance bounds are probabilistic, not worst-case. Redis uses a skip list internally for its sorted sets.

Real-world analogy: think of express trains and local trains. When travelling, you take the express (top level) as far as possible before switching to the local (lower levels) near your destination.

O(log n) avg.
