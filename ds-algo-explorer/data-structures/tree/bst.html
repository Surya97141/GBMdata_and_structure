Binary Search Tree

Every left child is smaller, every right child is larger — enabling O(log n) search.

A BST is like a sorted phone book organised as a tree. At every page (node) you either go left (earlier letters) or right (later letters) until you find the name you want. This halving of the search space is what gives the O(log n) average performance.

The BST property is strict: all values in a node's left subtree are less than the node's value, and all values in its right subtree are greater. This ordering is maintained on every insert and delete operation.

BSTs power database indexes, auto-complete engines, and ordered sets in standard libraries. However, without balancing the tree can degenerate into a linked list in the worst case — which is why AVL and Red-Black trees were invented.
