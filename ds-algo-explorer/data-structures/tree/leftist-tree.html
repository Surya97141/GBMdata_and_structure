Leftist Tree

A heap-ordered binary tree that intentionally leans left, enabling efficient O(log n) merging of two heaps.

A Leftist Tree (leftist heap) is a binary tree satisfying the min-heap property with an additional "leftist" bias: the null-path-length (npl) of the right subtree is always less than or equal to the left. The npl of a node is the length of the shortest path to a null descendant.

Imagine a company org chart where the "right spine" is always as short as possible. All the "weight" is pushed to the left side. This asymmetry is deliberate — it means merging two leftist heaps only ever touches the right spines (which are short, at most O(log n) nodes), making merge run in O(log n) time.

The key insight: every other heap operation — insert, extract-min — is implemented as a merge. Insert merges a single-node heap; extract-min removes the root and merges its two subtrees. The leftist bias ensures the right spine stays logarithmic.
