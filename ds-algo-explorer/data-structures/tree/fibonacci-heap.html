Fibonacci Heap

A lazy collection of min-heap trees offering the best known amortized bounds — the secret engine behind fast Dijkstra and Prim algorithms.

A Fibonacci Heap is a collection of min-heap-ordered trees linked in a circular doubly-linked list. It earns its name because a tree of rank k (degree k) has at least F(k+2) nodes, where F is the Fibonacci sequence. This structural guarantee keeps operations tight.

Think of it as a lazy assistant who manages your task pile. When you hand them a new task (insert) or merge two piles (union), they just toss it onto the list — no sorting at all, O(1). Only when you demand "what's the most urgent task?" (extract-min) do they actually consolidate and tidy the piles. This lazy batching is the source of the amortized speedup.

Decrease-key is particularly special: it runs in amortized O(1) by cutting a node from its tree and placing it in the root list, cascading cuts up if needed. This makes Fibonacci Heaps uniquely suited for graph algorithms where edge relaxation is frequent.

Insert
O(1) amortized
Extract Min
O(log n) amortized
Decrease Key
O(1) amortized
Union
O(1)
Use Cases
Dijkstra, Prim MST
