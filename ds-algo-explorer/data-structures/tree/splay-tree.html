Splay Tree

A self-adjusting BST that moves every accessed node to the root, making recently used elements faster to reach next time.

A Splay Tree is a self-organizing binary search tree. After every access — whether insert, search, or delete — the target node is moved to the root through a series of rotations called splaying. No extra balance information is stored; the tree restructures itself based purely on access patterns.

Think of a self-organizing bookshelf. Every time you read a book, you put it back at the very top. If you tend to re-read the same books, they stay near the top and are quickly accessible. Rarely read books drift toward the bottom. Over many accesses, the bookshelf optimizes itself for your reading habits — exactly what a splay tree does with data.

Three rotation cases: Zig — one rotation when the node's parent is the root. Zig-Zig — two rotations in the same direction when node and parent are both left (or both right) children. Zig-Zag — two rotations in opposite directions. The zig-zig case is key: it rotates the grandparent first, achieving better amortized bounds than a naive double rotation.
