Binary Heap

A complete binary tree satisfying the heap property — the fastest way to always have the minimum (or maximum) at your fingertips.

A Binary Heap is a complete binary tree stored as an array. In a min-heap, every parent is smaller than or equal to its children, so the root always holds the global minimum. In a max-heap, every parent is larger, keeping the maximum at the root.

Think of a hospital emergency room triage queue. Patients are sorted not by arrival time but by severity. The most critical patient (smallest severity score = most urgent) is always served first. When a new patient arrives, they "bubble up" to the right position; when the top patient leaves, the queue "sifts down" to restore order.

The array representation is elegant: for node at index i, its left child is at 2i+1, right child at 2i+2, and parent at ⌊(i-1)/2⌋. No pointers needed — pure index arithmetic.
